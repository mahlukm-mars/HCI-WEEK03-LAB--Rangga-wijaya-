Welcome to Indonesia - Land of 17,000 Islands

Indonesia, officially the Republic of Indonesia, is a country in Southeast Asia and Oceania between the Indian and Pacific oceans. It consists of over 17,000 islands, including Sumatra, Java, Sulawesi, and parts of Borneo and New Guinea.

Top Destinations

Indonesia offers diverse landscapes from pristine beaches in Bali to ancient temples in Java, from volcanic mountains in Sumatra to tropical rainforests in Kalimantan.

Must-Visit Places:

  • Bali: Famous for its beaches, temples, and vibrant culture
  • Yogyakarta: Cultural heart of Java with historic temples
  • Raja Ampat: World-class diving and marine biodiversity
  • Komodo Island: Home to the famous Komodo dragons
  • Lake Toba: Stunning volcanic lake in North Sumatra

Rich Cultural Heritage

Indonesia's cultural diversity is reflected in its hundreds of ethnic groups, languages, and traditions. From traditional dances like Kecak in Bali to the ancient art of batik, Indonesian culture offers endless discoveries.

The country's motto "Bhinneka Tunggal Ika" (Unity in Diversity) perfectly captures the harmonious coexistence of different cultures, religions, and traditions across the archipelago.

Quick Travel Tips

  • Best time to visit: April to October (dry season)
  • Visa on arrival available for many countries
  • Indonesian Rupiah (IDR) is the local currency
  • Bahasa Indonesia is the official language
  • Respect local customs and dress modestly at temples
